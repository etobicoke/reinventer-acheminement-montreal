⚜ Live demo · the deterministic resolver, on real requests

How the resolver identifies the asset, routes it with a reason it can cite, and redirects anything that isn't Montréal's to fix.

Every location below is a real request from the City's 311 open data, and the routing shown is the resolver's, and it matches the City's own decision. The photo/voice step is the input; this page demonstrates the part that makes routing trustworthy: a deterministic, citable resolver that never guesses a department.

A citizen sends a photo and a few words, spoken or typed: in Montréal, more than eight reports in ten still arrive by phone. Perception turns that into an asset + defect, and the voice is transcribed, then deleted. Then a deterministic resolver decides whose problem it is, and can always say why, or say "not ours."

Resolver → redirect to Ville de Mont-RoyalIn the City's data, interior Mont-Royal requests are never assigned to borough road work ✓
Mont-Royal is an enclave, a separate municipality entirely surrounded by Montréal. The pin lands inside its boundary, so the answer is "not ours" no matter what the photo shows. Geometry alone gets this right only if it knows the enclave exists; a category classifier never does.
