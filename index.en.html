Reinventing the routing

Resolve the asset, don't classify the photo. And prove it, figure by figure.
Olotalk municipal vertical · submission 2026-07-24 · live presentations Sep 16–17 · Olochat AI Inc.
A note from us.

We didn't start with a demo. We spent days inside Montréal's open 311 data, and the most useful thing we learned is that the obvious approach is the wrong one. Routing a report isn't about recognizing a pothole in a photo; it's about knowing whose problem it is. The City already answers that about 97% of the time, with rules you can recompute. So we built the version a juror can check.

Everything here comes from real data, and every figure regenerates from the City's own API. We'd rather you verify us than take our word for it.

Ta'ji Gustave Nganso · Founder, Olochat AI Inc.

The brief asks for AI that reads a photo and routes the request. We measured four years of Montréal's routing first, and routing turns out to be the easy part. The AI does the perception; a deterministic, citable resolver does the routing, defers to a human when the sources disagree, and never emits a department name, so it cannot misroute by hallucination.
